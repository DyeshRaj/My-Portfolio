The Engineer
Behind the Machine

I'm Dyesh Raj, a third-year B.Tech student in Instrumentation and Control Engineering at the National Institute of Technology, Tiruchirappalli — one of India's premier technical institutes.

My engineering identity lives at the intersection of two worlds: the precision of embedded systems and hardware design, and the elegance of software intelligence. I don't just write code or wire circuits — I build systems where both converge to solve real-world problems.

From designing thermal management circuits with MOSFETs to building real-time computer vision pipelines with OpenCV, from gesture-controlled interfaces via ESP32 to AI-powered product matching systems — my work spans the full stack from silicon to software.

I'm driven by a curiosity to automate intelligently — to create systems that sense, think, and act. Currently focused on automation, computer vision, and human-computer interaction.
